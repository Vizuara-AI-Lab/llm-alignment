Abstract

The proliferation of Large Language Models (LLMs) has unveiled their immense potential across diverse applications, yet consistently aligning their outputs with human values, safety guidelines, and intended objectives remains a critical challenge. Existing alignment strategies often struggle with scalability, generalizability, and the nuanced interpretation of complex ethical directives, leading to persistent issues such as hallucination, bias amplification, and the generation of harmful content. To address these limitations, we propose a novel adaptive alignment framework that seamlessly integrates self-correction mechanisms with dynamic value distillation from a lightweight, context-aware preference model. Our approach enables LLMs to autonomously refine their behavior based on real-time feedback and adapt to evolving ethical landscapes without extensive re-training. Preliminary evaluations demonstrate that our framework significantly reduces the incidence of toxic and biased outputs by over X%, simultaneously enhancing factual consistency by an average of Y% across benchmark datasets compared to state-of-the-art methods. Furthermore, the model exhibits a Z improvement in adherence to predefined safety protocols in adversarial scenarios. These results underscore the potential for more robust, trustworthy, and context-sensitive LLM deployment, paving the way for safer and more responsible AI systems that can operate reliably in complex real-world environments. This work represents a crucial step towards the practical realization of truly aligned artificial intelligence.
