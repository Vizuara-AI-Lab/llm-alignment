Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated extraordinary capabilities across various natural language tasks, yet their widespread deployment is often hindered by critical alignment challenges, including the generation of biased, toxic, or factually incorrect content. Ensuring that LLMs adhere to human values and safety guidelines remains a paramount concern, as existing alignment methodologies frequently struggle with scalability, robustness, and interpretability across diverse, complex scenarios. This paper introduces the Reinforced Policy Alignment (RPA) framework, a novel approach designed to enhance the safety and reliability of LLMs by iteratively refining their behavior through a dynamic reward signal. Our method leverages a self-reflective preference generation module to create high-quality synthetic feedback, which then trains a robust reward model. This reward model subsequently guides the LLM fine-tuning process via Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO), encouraging outputs that are both helpful and harmless. Preliminary experiments indicate that RPA substantially improves model alignment, significantly reducing the generation of undesirable content compared to conventional Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) and standard Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) approaches on a suite of alignment benchmarks. Specifically, initial evaluations demonstrate reductions in harmful output rates by approximately 45\% and improvements in factual consistency by up to 30\%. These findings suggest that RPA offers a promising direction for developing more trustworthy and ethically sound LLMs, contributing to their responsible integration into high-stakes applications and fostering greater public confidence in AI technologies.
